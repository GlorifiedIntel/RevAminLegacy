About Amin Zaigi Ngharen

In every generation, life often surprises people with the hatching of men, from seemingly inconsequential backgrounds, but whose births are destined to alter the course of history. Often from such relative obscurity, men of purpose are humbly raised, who will blaze new trajectories, shining their torches in life’s darkest and crude places, and birthing new hopes.

Birth

Rev., Prof. Amin Zaigi Ngharen was a pastor, speaker, and best-selling author of “The begging Church.” And “Give me this Mountain”. He served the people in Kings’ Summit a Congress WBN Kingdom Community in Akwanga, Nasarawa, Nigeria. His message for the hurting, the guilty, the lonely, and the discouraged is “God loves you.”

As a writer, Amin was known for combining historic, poetic storytelling and adrenalin power packed messages with the heart of a pastor, lecturer and historian. All of his best books began as sermon series at Hinterland then, and Kings’ Summit.

Amin’s books “The begging Church” and “Give me this mountain” had motivated lots of people and created in them the passion to win soul for the Kingdom of God on heart.

Monday, 23rd August, 1965, was an ordinary day. Just like any other, a shrilling sound of a newborn’s cry was heard. The baby born into the loving family of Princess Habiba and her beloved husband, Pa Zaigi fondly called "Zaki". Amin was the fourth of twelve children, of his parents, late Pa Zaigi and Princess Habiba Ngharen Zaigi, of Rija, Mada Central District of Akwanga Local Government Area in Nasarawa State. This son would live to survive many things that cut short the lives of others. And in due time, this son would emerge as a Professor of History, amongst many other accomplishments.

Education and Qualifactions

Amin attended the Sudan United Mission Primary School, Ancho B, between 1974 – 1980. Then gained admission into Government Secondary School, Wamba, where he studied from 1980 – 1985. He graduated as the best in his set. While in the secondary school, the young Amin started representing his school in debates, across Plateau state, (Akwanga, Wamba were under Plateau until the creation of Nasarawa State in 1996), where his eloquence was unrivalled. He won prizes as a debater. He was a prefect and debater from his third year in secondary school, till he graduated. He proceeded to the School of Preliminary Studies (SPS), Keffi, between 1985 – 1986. Again, he graduated at the top of his set.

Amin gained admission to University of Jos, Plateau State, in 1987 after graduating from SPS Keffi. He graduated from University of Jos in 1990. While studying History at the University of Jos, Amin also studied Theology at the famous International College of Ministries, Jos, and graduated successfully. During his one year mandatory service (NYSC), he served as the State Travelling Secretary with the Scripture Union (SU), in Ondo State. Because of his outstanding performance, he had two employment letters before his passing out parade. He, however, turned down the offers and returned home, where he believed his future is destined. Returning home from the NYSC Service, He got a job to lecture History at the College of Education, Akwanga.

Amin returned to University of Jos for his Masters between 1996 – 2000, then in 2007, he enrolled for a Postgraduate Diploma in Education, in Uthman Dan Fodio University, then in 2010. He returned to University of Jos for Ph.D and graduated in 2010 with Ph.D in History and International Studies. Amin taught History for twenty-four years in College of Education, Akwanga, and reached the peak of his career. He held various leadership positions at the College, including serving as, the Dean of Students Affairs, Director ICT; Staff Representative on the Governing Council; Chairman of the Academic Union; Chairman, Joint Staff Union of all Tertiary Institutions, Nasarawa State; and several others.

Life Experiences

While still in the service of his former employers, he was appointed Secretary to Akwanga Local Government Council. Amin was also appointed Chairman of Social Democratic Party SDP of former Akwanga Local Government Area. Amin’s sterling leadership qualities gave him national visibility from a very young age. He was appointed as a Member of Convention Planning Committee, of the SDP Primaries that produced the late MKO Abiola as the Party’s Presidential flag bearer.

He was appointed Director, Nasarawa State Water Board, between 2007 – 2009. He also served as the Chaplain, Chapel of Redemption College of Education, Akwanga, between 1996 -2000. He founded Kings’ Summit, Akwanga in 2001, which serves as a nexus for other Kingdom Communities. He was appointed one of the Global Oversight Coordinators of Congress WBN, a global network of related Kingdom Communities pioneering a new civilization.

He was appointed Chairman of the Committee that investigated the violent Students Riots in Nasarawa State University, Keffi. Amin has served the State and the nation in various capacities, including serving as the Team Leader that led Pilgrims to the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan, this year. He also performed several other assignments both for the nation and State at different times. He had the privilege of being coopted into the committee that produced the working document for Mr. President’s second term.

Amin has taught History at both NCE, undergraduate and postgraduate levels in College of Education Akwanga, Federal University of Lafia, University of Jos, Nasarawa State University, Keffi, and Plateau State University, Bokkos. He has graduated several undergraduates and postgraduate students, across the institutions.

Amin has served the Federal University of Lafia in several committees, including the University Survey Committee, Departmental Postgraduate Coordinator, Faculty of Arts Representative on the Postgraduate Board, Hall Adviser, Editor – Lafia Journal of History and International Development etc. Amin is a Proprietor of two schools, viz Summit Children Academy and Issachar High School, all in Akwanga

Achievements

Professor Amin Zaigi Ngharen has authored eight (8) books, Contributed Eight (8) Book chapters. He has also published over 30 journal articles in institutional and referred journals nationally and internationally. One of his papers won the Best Paper presentation Award in the University of Dar Esalam, Tanzania in 2016. He has researched intensively into cultural history, and authored extensively on the Middle Belt of Nigeria.

Professor Amin Zaigi Ngharen is a member of several academic associations, including Historical Society of Nigeria (HSN), History Teachers Association Colleges of Education (HISTANCE), Africa Research Programme, etc.

Professor Amin Zaigi Ngharen is also widely travelled. He has visited:
